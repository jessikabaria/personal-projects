Analysis & Recommendations

Key findings drawn from all 94 responses, with a prioritised set of actionable recommendations for the L&D team.

Key Findings
🤖
AI Learning is the Undisputed #1 Priority
91% selected AI Fluency & Agentic Tools — the highest of any category
86 of 94 respondents want AI training. Open text calls out Agentic AI, LLMs, Prompt Engineering, RAG, Claude, and AI for non-technical workflows. With 83% already Intermediate or above, advanced applied content is required — not introductions.
  • Multiple employees suggest making at least one AI module mandatory per quarter to create protected learning time.
  • Demand spans both technical (engineering, architecture) and non-technical roles (finance, communications).
  • Role-specific AI tracks would dramatically increase relevance and uptake.
⏱
Time & Unawareness Are Structural Barriers, Not Personal Ones
38% cite workload; 28% of non-participants didn't know offerings existed
Among the 43% who didn't participate, the top two barriers are workload (38%) and unawareness (28%). These are not motivational failures.
  • Time: L&D loses to urgent work without protected hours. Multiple employees explicitly requested mandatory or formally allocated learning time.
  • Unawareness: 35% don't know their POC. 10% don't know where to find any L&D information at all.
  • New joiners: 7 non-participants cited being new. No structured L&D onboarding exists — a clear gap with a straightforward fix.
📡
Discovery is Passive & Fragmented
No unified calendar, no new joiner intro, widespread platform confusion
Slack (73%) and The Landing (71%) are the top channels — but both rely on employees actively seeking updates. Communication averages only 3.5/5.
  • No unified monthly calendar of sessions — multiple employees explicitly requested one.
  • Documented confusion across OktaU, LearnLUX, and Cornerstone.
  • L&D has no formal presence in townhalls, team meetings, or product share-outs.
🏫
Format Gap: In-Person Most Wanted, Least Delivered
32% prefer in-person; demand outstrips supply, especially in India
In-person is the #1 preferred format yet complaints about its absence dominate open text, particularly for Bangalore.
  • Morning-only scheduling excludes shift-based teams — the Finance team on 2–11pm shift was directly named.
  • Micro-learning (11%) is growing — employees want 10–15 min snackable modules between meetings.
  • Self-paced learners want O'Reilly, Pluralsight, and KodeKloud — not just Udemy.
✅
62% Positive Impact — Strong Goodwill to Build On
The foundation is solid; the gaps are structural and fixable
62% agree L&D has contributed to their performance and growth. The volume and warmth of kudos — with specific named shoutouts — signals genuine organisational goodwill.
  • The 26% neutral group represents a conversion opportunity — not disengaged, just under-served.
  • The 13% negative group is concentrated among low-awareness, non-participating employees — fixable through better discovery and onboarding.
  • This goodwill gives the L&D team significant leverage to advocate for structural changes: protected time, certification funding, and platform expansion.
Prioritised Recommendations
1
Launch a Tiered AI Learning Track — Immediately
Build a structured AI curriculum: Foundations (AI literacy, responsible AI), Applied (Prompt Engineering, Claude, workflow automation), and Advanced (Agentic AI, LLMs, RAG). Offer in-person and self-paced formats. Consider one mandatory module per quarter. Create non-technical variants for Finance, HR, and Communications teams.
Urgent · High Impact
2
Publish a Monthly L&D Session Calendar on The Landing & Slack
Create and maintain a visible monthly calendar of all upcoming sessions. Pin it in #okta-learning. Automate a weekly Slack digest. This directly addresses the "discovery by accident" problem and was explicitly requested by multiple respondents.
Urgent · Quick Win
3
Create a Structured L&D Onboarding Pack for New Joiners
Send a clear document to all new joiners in their first week covering: who their L&D POC is, what each platform is for, a recommended 30/60/90-day learning path by role, and how to request training. 7 non-participants cited being new, and several open-text responses confirmed there is no structured L&D introduction for new joiners.
Urgent · Scalable Fix
4
Advocate for Protected Learning Time — Partner with HR & Leadership
The #1 barrier is workload. Partner with HR and senior leadership to pilot a monthly "Learning Hour" or quarterly "Learning Day." This was explicitly requested by multiple respondents including senior employees. Use this survey data to build the business case.
Urgent · Requires Leadership Buy-in
5
Increase In-Person Sessions in India With Multiple Time Slots
In-person is the #1 preferred format and the most under-delivered. Increase session cadence and offer at least two time slots — morning IST and afternoon IST — to accommodate shift-based workers. The Finance team on the 2–11pm shift was directly named as excluded by current scheduling.
High Priority
6
Resolve the OktaU / Cornerstone / LearnLUX Platform Confusion
Publish a clear, simple "Which Platform Should I Use?" guide and pin it in #okta-learning and The Landing. Explain what each platform is for, who it serves, and where mandatory vs optional content lives. This was raised by multiple respondents at different seniority levels.
High Priority
7
Introduce Micro-Learning Modules (10–15 min) for High-Demand Topics
Create short, high-impact modules for AI, Leadership, and Communication. Multiple employees — including highly engaged ones — say they cannot block 2 hours for a deep dive. 10–15 minute modules won't replace deep dives but will significantly increase overall learning frequency.
Medium Priority
8
Expand Platform Access: O'Reilly, Pluralsight or KodeKloud, Certification Reimbursement
Add O'Reilly for technical books (requested by 4+ respondents). Add at least one lab-based platform — Pluralsight or KodeKloud — for hands-on engineering skills. Establish a formal certification reimbursement policy for AWS, K8s, and Okta certifications. Low cost, high satisfaction impact.
Medium Priority
9
Build a Mentorship Directory & Peer Learning Programme
Create a Slack or Landing-based directory where employees register as mentors, declare available hours per month, and allow colleagues to book sessions. Peer-to-peer coaching is the 5th most preferred format and was explicitly requested in open text. A form + Slack channel could launch this quickly at very low cost.
Ongoing Investment
10
Build Role-Specific & Grade-Specific Learning Paths
Move beyond flat, one-size-fits-all offerings. Create distinct learning paths for Engineers, Engineering Managers, Business Function ICs, and New Joiners. One respondent directly stated "customise based on the grade you join." Personalised paths address both the relevance concern and the 26% neutral sentiment group who don't know where to start.
Longer-Term Investment
