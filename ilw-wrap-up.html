What Comes Next

This Is Version One.

The feedback isn't a report card — it's a brief. Five clear signals from participants that tell us exactly where ILW's second edition can go further.

1
Remove the Friction Before People Feel It
OpenWeb UI access issues cost us momentum mid-session. A ten-minute platform check the day before would eliminate this entirely. Friction at the start of a session shapes how the whole thing lands.
2
Design for the Range of the Room
Our participants ranged from first-week joiners to practitioners with years of context. A beginner and an advanced pathway — especially for AI sessions — means no one is bored and no one is lost.
3
Let Them Build, Not Just Watch
The most consistent signal in the feedback: people wanted to make something. A build-alongside format — where facilitators build live and participants mirror — will drive sharply higher confidence and value scores.
4
Send People Away With Something They Can Use
Slides, recordings, a one-page reference. Multiple participants asked for these. A post-session resource pack takes twenty minutes to prepare and creates value that compounds for weeks after the day itself.
5
Give the AI Academy a Map
The Academy is well-built — what it needs is a clear entry point per level and a "New" tag for fresh content. Right now, new joiners don't know where to start. A simple learning pathway solves it immediately.
