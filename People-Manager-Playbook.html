Performance Management

Okta's performance framework is built on clarity and accountability. Performance outcomes should never come as a surprise to an employee.

The OAR Framework — Objectives, Achievements, Results

OARs (Okta Alignment Reviews) are the core performance tool. Every employee documents three components each review cycle. The manager's role is to coach quality into each one.

O
Component 1
Objectives
  • Clear, high-level goal set at the start of the cycle
  • Aligned with team and company priorities
  • Ambitious and impact-focused
Manager's Role

Help employees set clear, impactful objectives. Ask employees to draft their own goals first — this creates immediate ownership.

A
Component 2
Achievements
  • Specific actions and projects used to pursue the Objective
  • Describes the "how" — effort and approach
  • Should be concise and clearly written
Manager's Role

Guide team members to be specific, not vague. Vague achievements make it impossible to assess genuine contribution.

R
Component 3
Results
  • Measurable, tangible outcomes of the Achievements
  • Quantifiable and directly linked to the Objective
  • Answers the "so what"
Manager's Role

Assist and challenge the team to quantify their impact. If a result cannot be measured, it needs to be reframed.

Review Cycles — Two Checkpoints Per Year
H1
Progress Check-in
First half of the year — informal and developmental
  • Informal review focused on progress, feedback, and development
  • No final performance levels are assigned at this stage
  • Purpose is to course-correct early, not to evaluate
  • Conversations should be candid and forward-looking
H2
Final Review
Second half of the year — formal and calibrated
  • Full-year evaluation covering all OAR components
  • Culminates in Performance Calibrations
  • Final performance levels are determined and documented
  • Outcomes inform compensation, promotion, and development plans
Managing Low Performance — Step by Step
1

Provide Timely Feedback

Address performance concerns as soon as they emerge. Do not save critical feedback for the annual review.

2

Set Clear Expectations

Communicate specific and measurable improvement expectations. Document every coaching conversation.

3

Engage Your PBP

If performance does not improve after coaching, or if a formal process is being considered, contact your PBP immediately.

Do not manage a PIP or termination independentlyPBP partnership is mandatory from the moment a formal performance process is being considered. Acting unilaterally creates legal and compliance risk for Okta and for you.

The Three Pillars of a Valid Promotion Case

All three criteria must be satisfied. A strong case on two out of three is not sufficient to proceed.

1
Business Need

There must be a sustained, long-term business need for the role at the higher level, supported by available budget. A temporary surge in workload does not qualify.

Required
2
Increased Scope

The employee must demonstrate a meaningful and measurable increase in scope, complexity, and impact — not simply doing the same job for longer.

Required
3
Individual Readiness

Sustained performance at the next level, already demonstrated. A "Doing Great Work (Strong)" rating is the minimum prerequisite.

Required

A Promotion Is

  • Evidence of already operating at the next level
  • Tied to a genuine business need and expanded scope
  • A forward-looking investment, not a backward-looking reward
  • Aligned to the annual promotion cycle and ARP process

A Promotion Is Not

  • A reward for past hard work — use bonuses and merit increases for this
  • A retention tool for someone who is a flight risk
  • Something that can be guaranteed before calibration
  • Justified by tenure or loyalty alone
Skills-Based vs. Title-Based Growth
An important reframe for IndiaIn the India tech market, title progression is often seen as the only visible sign of growth. Managers must proactively reframe what growth looks like beyond a job level change.
Formal Path
Title-Based Growth
  • Promotion to the next job level
  • Formal expansion of scope and authority
  • Movement to a higher compensation band
  • Requires all three promotion pillars to be met
Continuous Path
Skills-Based Growth
  • Deepening technical expertise in a domain
  • Taking on cross-functional ownership
  • Leading strategic projects without a title change
  • Building influence across teams and functions
Common Promotion Challenges

A common mistake is building a promotion case around what an employee completed — a list of projects and tasks — rather than how they approached them at the next level. A strong case demonstrates strategic influence, cross-functional leadership, and a measurable step up in complexity and ownership. Completing expected work is not sufficient justification for promotion.

Advocating for promotions without a true business need creates a top-heavy team structure and sets unsustainable expectations. Before building a promotion case, confirm there is a genuine organisational need at the higher level and that budget is available. Promotions must be driven by business logic, not by the manager's desire to reward or retain.

Compensation & Rewards

Managers own the relationship with the employee on compensation topics. The goal is to connect performance clearly to rewards — with honesty, preparation, and empathy.

Compensation Structure in India
Fixed
Base Salary

The fixed monthly cash component. Determined through rigorous market analysis specific to the India technology sector. Increases are tied to performance reviews and the annual comp cycle.

Variable
Annual Bonus

A variable cash incentive tied to both individual performance and company-level results.

Bonuses are not guaranteed. They reflect performance against defined targets in a given year.
Long-Term
Equity — RSUs

A long-term incentive in the form of Restricted Stock Units. RSUs vest over time and are a significant component of total compensation in India's competitive tech talent market.

Four Principles for Compensation Conversations
01
Own the Message

Deliver the outcome directly and with confidence. Do not defer to "HR" or "the system." You are the manager — own it.

02
Be Prepared

Know the numbers, understand the philosophy, and anticipate the questions before entering the conversation.

03
Connect to Impact

Link every compensation outcome to the employee's performance and the "why." Employees accept outcomes more readily when they understand the reasoning.

04
Listen More

Give the employee space to process and respond. Do not over-explain. Silence after a difficult message is normal — allow it.

POSH — Non-Negotiable Manager Duties

The Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act is Indian law. The following four duties are non-negotiable for every manager.

Model Professionalism

Consistently model and foster a respectful, professional environment within your team. The standard starts with you.

Report — Do Not Investigate

Upon observing or hearing of any potential harassment, report to your PBP immediately. Do not attempt to investigate, validate, or mediate in any way.

Confidentiality Has Limits

You cannot promise absolute confidentiality to anyone who raises a concern. There is a legal duty to report all concerns to appropriate internal channels.

Prevent Retaliation

Ensure the work environment remains free from retaliation against anyone who raises a concern or participates in an investigation. Retaliation is a serious violation of Okta policy.

Leave & Attendance
Track in Workday

Approve and track all leave requests through Workday — the system of record for all employee data.

Statutory Leave Types

Familiarise yourself with India's statutory leave policies: Earned Leave (EL) and Sick Leave (SL). Non-compliance creates legal risk.

Leave of Absence (LoA)

Understand Okta's Leave of Absence policy and the process for initiating one. Contact your PBP or People Operations for guidance.

Culture, Communication & Engagement

Effective management in India requires awareness of local cultural context. The guidance here provides helpful framing — not stereotypes. India is extraordinarily diverse.

What Motivates Employees in India
Motivator 01
Visible Recognition

Public acknowledgement of good work matters significantly. Do not assume the team knows their contributions are valued — say it explicitly and often.

Motivator 02
Growth Opportunities

Employees in India's tech market expect a clear and active path to growth. When the path is unclear, attrition risk increases quickly.

Motivator 03
Skill Development

Access to learning, certifications, and new technical challenges is a strong retention driver. Invest in it proactively.

Motivator 04
Manager Advocacy

Employees want to feel their manager is actively championing their interests — in promotion cycles, compensation reviews, and cross-functional visibility.

Building Trust in Distributed Teams
Protect the 1:1

Schedule regular, recurring one-to-one meetings and protect that time. Cancelling 1:1s repeatedly signals to employees that they are low priority.

Camera On — Default

Default to camera-on for internal team meetings. It builds personal connection, aids in reading non-verbal cues, and increases engagement. Managers model this behaviour first.

Share Strategic Context

Regularly share information about broader company and team strategy. Employees who understand the "why" behind their work are more engaged and more resilient to change.

Cross-Geography Operating Norms
Meeting Equity

Rotate meeting times so that the burden of early-morning or late-night calls does not always fall on the India team. This is a fairness issue, not just a scheduling preference.

A Culture of Documentation

Meeting notes, project decisions, and key updates must be stored in a central, accessible location. Time zones should never be a barrier to critical information.

Clarity on Communication Tools

Set explicit expectations for which tools are used for which purpose. For example: Slack for urgent day-to-day queries; email for formal updates and records.

WFH & Hybrid Expectations
Define Core Hours

Set clear expectations for when team members are expected to be online and responsive. This must be explicit — not assumed.

Response Time Standards

Establish clear norms for how quickly team members should respond on Slack and email. Communicate these expectations consistently.

Outcome-Based Performance

Performance standards and quality of deliverables remain consistent regardless of where someone works. Physical location does not change the standard expected.

Retention & Attrition Management

Proactive retention is significantly more effective than reactive responses to resignation. Address the root causes before employees start looking.

India-Specific Attrition Triggers
Trigger 01
Perceived Lack of Career Growth

When employees cannot see a clear and active path to promotion or advancement, they look externally. This is the most common attrition trigger in India's tech market.

Trigger 02
Compensation Below Market

India's technology sector is highly competitive on pay. If compensation falls meaningfully below market benchmarks, employees will receive and consider competing offers.

Trigger 03
Insufficient Scope & Ownership

High performers seek greater impact, ownership, and scope. When they feel underutilised or constrained, engagement drops rapidly.

Stay Interview Guidance
What is a stay interview?A proactive, structured conversation designed to understand what keeps an employee engaged at Okta — and what might cause them to leave. Unlike an exit interview, the goal is retention. Conduct these before someone is considering leaving, not after.
  • When a high performer shows signs of disengagement
  • When an employee asks repeated questions about career progression or compensation
  • After a significant org or role change
  • After a promotion or compensation outcome that disappointed the employee

Escalate to your PBP as soon as you identify a risk. Do not wait until you are certain. Key signals include:

  • A sudden or sustained drop in engagement or output
  • Expressed dissatisfaction with career progression
  • Repeated questions about market pay or competing roles
Proactive Retention vs. Reactive Counter-Offers
DimensionProactive Retention StrategyReactive Counter-Offer
When It HappensBefore the employee has resigned or started lookingAfter the employee has formally resigned and presents a competing offer
Nature of ConversationHolistic and forward-looking — focused on aspirations, growth, and impactTransactional and backward-looking — focused almost entirely on matching compensation
Likely OutcomeA mutually agreed plan that reinforces the employee's sense of value and directionA temporary fix. Root causes remain unaddressed. Team equity is often damaged.
Manager Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Provide regular, specific, and actionable feedback throughout the year
  • Partner with your PBP on all formal ER and performance actions
  • Publicly recognise and celebrate team successes
  • Schedule regular 1:1 meetings and treat them as protected time
  • Be aware of the official Okta India holiday schedule
  • Rotate meeting times fairly across time zones

Don't

  • Wait until the annual review to deliver critical feedback
  • Attempt to manage a PIP or termination without PBP partnership
  • Assume the team knows their contributions are appreciated
  • Cancel 1:1 meetings frequently or treat them as optional
  • Always schedule meetings at times that create a late-hour burden for India
  • Ignore regional cultural observances and festival calendars

Crisis & Sensitive Situations

Step-by-step guidance for handling sensitive situations professionally, ensuring compliance, safety, and genuine employee support.

Universal rule for every crisisEmployee safety comes first. Document the situation immediately. Follow company policy on leave, pay, and support. Escalate to your PBP and Site Leadership. Keep all communications professional, empathetic, and documented.

Immediate Escalation Required
Contact PBP + Site Leadership Now

  • Threats, violence, or workplace safety concerns
  • Medical emergencies at the workplace
  • Any situation involving a risk to mental health or personal safety
  • Data breaches or compliance violations
Proactive Contact
Contact Your PBP Before It Escalates

  • Promotion and levelling questions
  • Compensation positioning concerns
  • Identified retention risks or potential resignations
  • Behavioural or conduct concerns
  • Team conflict affecting performance
Situation-by-Situation Guidance
1

Confirm Safety

Verify the employee is receiving immediate medical attention. All work considerations are secondary.

2

Notify Your PBP

Contact your PBP immediately. They will activate all necessary internal support systems.

3

Cover the Work

Reassign the employee's critical tasks and reassure the employee that their work is handled.

When an employee faces a serious illness, accident, or bereavement in their immediate family, the manager's role is to provide immediate and unconditional flexibility.

  • The priority is to allow the employee to focus entirely on their family without worry about work
  • Notify your PBP promptly to activate appropriate leave and support mechanisms
  • Document the situation and follow Okta's leave and pay policies throughout
  • Check in with the employee compassionately — do not pressurise on timelines

A manager's role in mental health situations is to be a supportive first point of contact — not a therapist or counsellor.

  • Create a safe, non-judgmental environment where the employee feels heard
  • Guide the employee toward confidential, professional resources
  • Do not attempt to diagnose, counsel, or resolve the situation yourself
  • For any situation involving a risk to safety, escalate to your PBP and Site Leadership immediately

Do

  • Receive the news professionally and calmly
  • Acknowledge the employee's decision without debate
  • Notify your PBP immediately — before making any agreements
  • Discuss notice period and handover requirements only after PBP guidance

Don't

  • Become defensive or take the decision personally
  • Make unilateral arrangements on early release without consulting your PBP
  • Independently initiate a counter-offer discussion

India has a rich and diverse calendar of public holidays and regional festivals. Managers are expected to:

  • Know the official Okta India public holiday schedule and plan team capacity accordingly
  • Demonstrate genuine sensitivity to regional cultural observances, even those not on the official schedule
  • Avoid scheduling critical deliverables or reviews around major festival periods without advance notice and consent

Knowledge Transfer — Cross-Geography Best Practices
Documentation First

In distributed environments, knowledge transfer must be deliberate and written. Verbal-only handoffs create gaps that become apparent only when someone is absent or unavailable.

Async-Friendly Design

Build in buffer time for asynchronous review and questions. Do not assume real-time clarification is always possible across time zones.

Centralised Repository

Store all KT artefacts — process documents, handover notes, project context — in a single, accessible location that any team member can find regardless of time zone.

Learning, Upskilling & Internal Transfers
Manager's Role in Development
  • Have regular career conversations to understand individual aspirations
  • Identify skill gaps relative to the employee's current and future goals
  • Approve relevant training, certifications, and learning opportunities
  • Assign stretch projects that allow employees to build and demonstrate new competencies
Internal Mobility — Eligibility

Okta encourages internal mobility. Managers must be supportive of employees who explore internal moves. However, an employee is not eligible to transfer or be promoted if any of the following apply:

  • Currently on a PIP, or completed one in the prior six months
  • Most recent performance rating was "Performance Improvement Required"
  • Has an active disciplinary action — Verbal, Written, or Final Written Warning

An "Inconsistent Performance" rating or an active misconduct investigation may also affect eligibility — assessed case-by-case by ER&C and the relevant PBP.

Hiring & Workforce Planning

Understanding India's talent market dynamics is essential for building effective teams and planning hiring timelines accurately.

India Talent Market — Key Realities
Market Reality 01
Highly Competitive

There is intense competition for top technology talent in India, particularly in niche or specialised skill areas. Top candidates typically run multiple interview processes simultaneously.

Market Reality 02
Long Notice Periods

Notice periods in India are typically 60–90 days. Okta's standard is two months. These must be factored into annual headcount and project resource planning — a hire today is not available for nearly three months.

Market Reality 03
Title & Compensation Sensitivity

There is a strong cultural emphasis on title progression in India's tech market. Compensation-driven movement is common in certain skill segments. Early-career professionals change roles frequently.

Speed and engagement matterDelayed interview feedback reduces the probability of offer acceptance. Candidate experience during the process directly impacts Okta's employer brand. Clear role definition at the outset reduces early attrition.
Offer Drop-off & Acceptance Risks
Common Reasons for Offer Drop-off
  • Counter-offers from the candidate's existing employer
  • Multiple competing offers being evaluated simultaneously
  • Delays between offer acceptance and the joining date
What Managers Can Do
  • Personally connect with final candidates after the offer stage
  • Maintain regular, warm engagement during the notice period
  • Ensure role scope and expectations are clearly defined from the start — ambiguity drives early attrition
First-Time Manager Transition — IC to Manager

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Continuing to operate as the strongest individual contributor
  • Micromanaging to maintain quality control
  • Avoiding difficult or uncomfortable conversations
  • Overcompensating with authority to establish credibility
  • Favouring former close peers unfairly

What to Do Instead

  • Shift focus to enabling the team's performance
  • Trust your team and delegate genuinely
  • Have direct, empathetic conversations early and often
  • Establish new professional boundaries fairly with all team members
  • Build a regular check-in cadence with your PBP from day one

Resignations & Exits

Departure is a natural part of the employee lifecycle. How a manager handles it shapes the employee's lasting impression of Okta and affects remaining team members.

Handling Resignations Professionally
Immediate Actions — What to Do

Acknowledge and Accept

Listen to the employee's reason for departure without debate, argument, or counter-pressure.

Notify Your PBP Immediately

Before making any commitments regarding exit timeline, final duties, or early release, contact your PBP.

Discuss Transition — With PBP Guidance

Once PBP guidance has been received, clarify the last working day, handover expectations, and knowledge transfer requirements.

Actions to Avoid
  • Becoming defensive, emotional, or taking the decision personally
  • Making unilateral decisions on early release without PBP consultation
  • Independently initiating a counter-offer discussion before speaking to your PBP
  • Sharing the news with the wider team before the employee has communicated it themselves
Notice Period & Handover
Standard NP
Two-Month Notice Period

Okta's contractual notice period in India is two months. Employees are obligated to serve their full notice period unless an alternative arrangement is agreed in writing, in consultation with the PBP.

Early Release
Subject to Business Discretion

Early release from the notice period is possible but is subject to business discretion and PBP consultation. This decision must never be made unilaterally by the manager.

Ownership
Knowledge Transfer Plan

The manager is responsible for creating and overseeing a structured handover plan. Ensure that no critical knowledge or context is lost during the transition period.
